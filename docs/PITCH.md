# Katiba OS pitch narrative

## Communication job

By the end, hackathon judges should select Katiba OS because it combines visible social impact with a credible technical and business path, while treating legal AI as accountable infrastructure rather than a generic chatbot.

## Slide story

### 1. Katiba OS turns scattered evidence into accountable legal action

A unified legal operating system for East Africa. Start with Kenyan small claims; expand through contracts, compliance, and evidence.

### 2. The legal problem is not a lack of answers—it is a broken path to action

A citizen has facts in messages, receipts, invoices, and memory. A legal professional must reconstruct those facts, find gaps, check sources, and prepare documents. Generic chat does not preserve provenance or create a review workflow.

### 3. Our flagship case goes from four source items to one reviewable pack

Amina’s invoice, payment, message, and delivery note become an evidence-linked chronology, missing-fact checklist, vetted legal sources, and a professional review queue.

### 4. One trust layer powers four legal engines

- Justice: plain-language intake and small-claims preparation
- Contract: clause risk, obligations, and health scoring
- Compliance: controls, deadlines, and audit readiness
- Evidence: checksums, provenance, chronology, and custody events

### 5. AI organizes; rules constrain; humans decide

The model receives scoped evidence, returns schema-validated facts, and cannot create legal sources. Deterministic fallback preserves the workflow. Lawyer/paralegal approval remains mandatory.

### 6. The prototype is already end-to-end

Responsive PWA, three roles, secure API, normalized data, explainable analysis, evidence mapping, PDF generation, contract review, analytics, security center, and automated tests.

### 7. Access to justice creates impact; professional workflows sustain the platform

Pilot the Justice Engine with legal-aid clinics. Sell Contract and Compliance workspaces to SMEs and legal teams. Use paid workflows to support assisted and pro-bono access.

### 8. A focused pilot can prove value quickly

Pilot with one legal-aid clinic and one SME legal team. Measure intake completion, time to review, missing-fact resolution, preparation turnaround, and reviewer confidence. The decision sought: pilot access, legal-content partners, and technical mentorship.

## Product metrics for a pilot

Do not claim these as current outcomes; use them as measurement targets:

- median minutes from intake to review-ready draft;
- percentage of users completing intake without live assistance;
- percentage of missing facts resolved before professional review;
- professional review minutes per case or contract;
- percentage of AI findings accepted, edited, or rejected;
- accessibility task-completion rate on low-end mobile devices;
- successful offline-to-online sync rate;
- zero cross-tenant access or unreviewed filing events.

## Business model hypothesis

- Free/assisted Justice Engine access through legal-aid and public-interest partners
- Per-seat or per-document Contract and Compliance plans for SMEs
- Organization plans for law firms, clinics, and in-house legal teams
- Integration revenue for e-signature, payments, registry, and document systems

The sequence matters: prove the small-claims workflow first, then scale the shared trust layer—not four disconnected products at once.
